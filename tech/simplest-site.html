The simplest website

September 18, 2023

I've often felt, as someone who claims to be a software engineer that any personal website I have needs to be the perfect representation of my skills; well designed, with my favorite framwork of the hour, well-selected component libraries, clever graphics, and each element perfectly designed to be reusable.

However, I realized some time ago that this drive for perfection was restraining me from making a website at all. Afterall, I have a job that I enjoy, that generally feels meaningful, and when I have an afternoon where I feel like spending time making well-crafted software, I figure I might as well contribute to depmap.org or a similar project where I can make a meaningful impact. I'm not inclined to spend my free moments polishing the one piece of software that no one's likely to see. And so my personal website has continued to languish over the years.

I'm restarting it today and seeing if this time, it might stick. The one change being that this will be the simplest website I've ever made. Almost entirely HTML and CSS, unless I really get a wild hair - and more focused on the content than anything else.
